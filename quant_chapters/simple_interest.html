Question 1

A person deposits $2000 in a bank for 3 years at 10% interest rate. Find the amount the person will obtain after 3 years.

Explanation

Step 1: Interest every year = Principal*(Interest /100) ==> $2000 * 0.1 ==> $200

Step 2: Interest received every year will be the same in simple interest.

Step 3: Interest received in 3 years = amount received in one year * 3 ==> $200 * 3 ==> $600

Step 4: Amount after 3 years = Principal + interest received in 3 years ==> $2600

Question 2

A person borrows $50000 from a bank for 5 years. He pays $66000 as simple interest. Find the rate of interest at which he borrowed the money.

Explanation

Step 1: Interest payed every year will be the same.

Step 2: Interest to pay per year = Total Interest/Number of years. ==> 66000/5 ==> $13200

Step 3: Interest each year = Principal*(Rate of interest /100) ==> 13200 = (66000/100)* Rate of interest

Step 4: ==> Rate of interest = 20%

Question 3

A certain amount of money invested at simple interest, doubles in 10 years. Find the rate of interest

Explanation

Step 1: Let us assume that the principal is $x.

Step 2: After 5 years the amount becomes $ 2*x.

Step 3: ==> 2x = x + Interest Earned

Step 4: Interest Earned in 5 years = $x ==> Interest earned every year = $x/5

Step 5: Principal * Rate of interest/100 = x/5 ==> x* Rate of interest = 20*x ==> Rateo of interest = 20%
