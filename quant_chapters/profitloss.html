Question 1

Salesman sold a pen at $750 at 10% loss then find the selling price to make Profit of 10%.

  1. $816
  2. $900
  3. $916
  4. $833

Explanation

Step 1: When we sell at $750 we incur a 10% loss implies that we are selling at, CP - 10% of CP ==>90% of CP.

Step 2: 0.9*CP=750 ==> CP=$833.33

Step 3: For Selling at 10% at profit the SP should be CP+10% of CP ==> 110% of CP.

Step 4: SP for 10% Profit = 1.1*CP ==> $916.33

Question 2

CP of pen is $320. Then find the increase in SP if Profit % is increased from 8% to 13%.

  1. $16
  2. $90
  3. $50
  4. $8

Explanation

Step 1: We need to find the difference in SP in both scenarios.

Step 2: In scenario 1, 8% profit implies SP of CP + 8% of CP ==> 1.08 * CP.

Step 3: In scenario 2, 13% profit implies SP of CP + 13% of CP ==> 1.13 * CP.

Step 4: 1.13* CP -1.08* CP ==> 0.05*CP ==> $16

Question 3

Two successive discounts of 10% and 15% are equivalent to a single discount of what % ?

  1. 20%
  2. 23.5%
  3. 15%
  4. 25%

Explanation

Step 1: Let us assume that the CP is $x.

Step 2: After first discount of 10% the CP becomes, $x - 10% of $x ==> 0.9*x.

Step 3: After second discount of 15% the CP becomes, $0.9*x - 15% of $0.9*x ==> 0.765 *x.

Step 4: Before the CP was $x and after discount CP is $ 0.765*x.

Step 5: Single equivalent discount ==> (x-0.765x)/x. ==>0.235 ==> 23.5% discount.
